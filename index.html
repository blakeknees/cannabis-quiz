Question #1:

What is the Endocannabinoid System?

Answer: The system inside our body

The endocannabinoid system is a molecular system responsible for regulating and balancing many processes in the body, including immune response, communication between cells, appetite and metabolism, memory, and more. It is through this system of receptors and metabolic enzymes that cannabinoids interact with the human body and trigger beneficial effects.

Question #2

What is a cannabinoid?

Answer: Compounds found in cannabis

Cannabinoids are naturally occurring compounds found in the plant. Over 480 different compounds present in the plant, and we have only identified just over 60. The most well-known cannabinoids are THC and CBD.

Question #3

What does THC stand for?

Answer: Tetrahydrocannabinol

Tetrahydrocannabinol is the main psychoactive compound in cannabis that produces the ‘high’ sensation.

Question #4

What does CBD stand for?

Answer: Cannabidiol

The second most commonly used cannabinoid found in the cannabis plant. Unlike THC, it's not as psychoactive, but does have equally therapeutic abilities: anti-oxidant, anti-inflammatory, neuroprotection, anti-nausea, and anti-anxiety. When taken in conjunction with THC in the same dose, the two cannabinoids work synergistically and can act as an excellent pain reliever.

Question #5

What are terpenes?

Answer: Essential oils produced by the plant

Terpenes are developed in the trichomes - shiny, sticky, mushroom-shaped resinous glands found on cannabis’ flowers and leaves. These naturally occurring chemical compounds act as a defense system for the plant against pests and predators, and are also responsible for its uniquely distinct aromas and flavours.

Question #6:

What is the ‘entourage effect’?

Answer: The synergistic act of combining cannabinoids

Refers to the various benefits different ratios of cannabinoids and terpenes produce. For example, when THC is used in conjunction with CBD, they can produce greater relief than if either cannabinoid were used in isolation.

Question #7:

What is hash?

Answer: Type of cannabis concentrate

Hash, aka: hashish, is the term for kief (extracted trichomes) that has been compacted into a taffy-like brick. It can be smoked or vaporized. Hash has a long history of usage in countries such as Lebanon, Afghanistan, India, Iran, Morocco, and Pakistan.

Question #8:

Which country was the first to legalize recreational cannabis?

Answer: Uruguay

Uruguay made history in 2013 when they became the first country to fully legalize cannabis for recreational use.
